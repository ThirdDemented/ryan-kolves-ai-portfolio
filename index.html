01

Precision Transfer System

Turning a high-risk hospital handoff into a coordinated operating model.

System designed

Problem

A critical transfer exposed fragmented ownership across physician acceptance, receiving-facility communication, regulatory documentation, timestamps, and bedside handoffs.

System

Mapped the end-to-end workflow and designed a transfer packet, readiness checklist, role cards, timestamp summary, escalation path, rapid debrief, and 24–48 hour follow-up.

Evidence

Created clear Team Lead, Communications, and Documentation roles so the workflow could be executed, taught, reviewed, and reused.

  • Workflow discovery
  • Cross-functional research
  • Process architecture
  • Regulatory risk
  • Playbook design
02

Critical Staffing Operations

Preserving advanced clinical capability under severe staffing constraints.

Live-use procedures

Problem

A rural EMS system needed to maintain advanced life support coverage across three crews and two bases despite recurring paramedic shortages.

System

Designed a constraint-aware ALS chase model, BLS-first procedures, dispatch criteria, intercept logic, documentation standards, move-up rules, and staff-facing guidance.

Evidence

Procedures were developed for real staffing dates and operating conditions across a 600-square-mile service area.

  • Constraint analysis
  • Decision support
  • Business continuity
  • Change communication
  • Operational safety
03

Operational Intelligence

Converting raw activity into decisions leaders can use.

Reports produced

Problem

Leaders lacked a concise, repeatable view of call activity, service mix, staffing pressure, notable incidents, fleet readiness, and emerging risks.

System

Developed a weekly operations brief and dashboard framework covering base-level volume, ALS/BLS activity, transports, call categories, staffing, fleet status, and action items.

Evidence

Produced recurring summaries from live operating data, corrected source counts, and left unsupported fields blank rather than manufacturing conclusions.

  • Operational analytics
  • Data quality
  • Executive reporting
  • Output calibration
  • Reusable templates
04

AI-Assisted Quality Review

Building a clinically grounded method for evaluating EMS documentation.

Working prototype

Problem

Traditional chart review is time-intensive and can miss contradictions among the complaint, assessment, protocol, treatment, structured fields, and narrative.

System

Developed a structured review rubric for clinical reasoning, documentation quality, internal consistency, protocol alignment, structured accuracy, and coaching feedback.

Evidence

Calibrated the workflow against real report structures and corrected the AI when information documented in the narrative was initially misclassified as missing.

  • AI evaluation
  • Prompt refinement
  • Human oversight
  • Quality assurance
  • Domain calibration
05

Evidence Drift Lab

A human-supervised adaptive loop for keeping AI conclusions inside their evidence.

Live adaptive demo

Problem

AI can combine true statements, silently promote an inference into fact, and repeat the drift until it appears authoritative.

System

Built a bounded extractor–evaluator–critic–optimizer loop that classifies claims, challenges unsupported certainty, revises overreach, and exposes its stopping reason.

Evidence

The working browser prototype accepts human correction, updates explainable class weights, persists lessons locally, and visibly changes future scoring without transmitting user text.

  • Adaptive evaluation
  • Recursive critique
  • Human feedback
  • Persistent state
  • Evidence provenance
  • Privacy by design
